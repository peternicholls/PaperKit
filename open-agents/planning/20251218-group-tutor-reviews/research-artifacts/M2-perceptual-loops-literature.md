### 2.2 Predictive Coding in Visual Sequences

**Source:** PMC8599921 - Visual cortex prediction error study  
**Relevance:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Pattern violation responses)

**Key Findings:**
- **Design:** Fixed sequence (standard) vs. deviant sequences (order altered)
- **Measurements:** V1 and V4 responses in macaques, EEG in humans
- **Result:** Little evidence for pattern-violation signals in passive viewing
- **Exception:** Human EEG showed violation response when explicitly instructed to detect patterns
- **Repetition suppression:** Responses weaken with repeated exposure (expectation strengthens)

**Key Implications:**
- **Passive viewing:** Pattern violations DON'T automatically trigger strong responses
- **Explicit attention:** Violations noticed when task requires pattern monitoring
- **Statistical learning:** Hundreds/thousands of repetitions needed for strong expectations
- **Time scale:** Our study (hundreds of trials) vs. long-term learning (days/weeks)

**Relevance to M√∂bius:**
- **First loop:** Not enough repetition to establish strong expectation
- **Twisted return:** May not feel like "violation" unless explicitly attended
- **Design choice:** M√∂bius twist is design feature, not perceptual error
- **Implication:** "Return with difference" is exploration, not expectation violation

**Priority:** HIGH - Reframes M√∂bius as **novelty** rather than **violation**
